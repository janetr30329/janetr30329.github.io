What is a Hedgehog?

A hedgehog is any of the spiny mammals of the subfamily Erinaceinae, in the eulipotyphlan family Erinaceidae. There are seventeen species of hedgehog in five genera found through parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa, and in New Zealand by introduction.

Life span: 2 - 5 years

Fun Facts

1. A group of hedgehogs is called an "array."

2. Hedgehogs are illegal in some parts of the United States.

3. A hedgehog has between 5000 and 7000 quills.

4. There are 17 different species of hedgehogs, non of which are native to America.

Hedgehog Care Tips:

*Clear eyes: The hog should appear alert; the eyes should not be crusty, sunken, or swollen.

*Clean fur and quills: While some anointing is normal (see below), fecal matter around the anus may indicate diarrhea or other underlying health issues.

*Hold the hedgehog every day as you let it get used to you. This may be as simple as placing it in your lap and talking to it. Encourage trust by giving it treats by hand, and add an old t-shirt you've worn for a day to its enclosure so it gets accustomed to your smell.
